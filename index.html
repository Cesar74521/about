General Information

Geography

Spain is a transcontinental country located in southwestern Europe and North Africa. In addition to occupying most of the Iberian Peninsula, Spain is also made up of two islands (the Canary Islands in the Atlantic Ocean and the Balearic Islands in the Mediterranean) and two autonomous cities in North Africa (Ceuta and Melilla) by composition. From several smaller islands in the area as well as other islands near the peninsula, such as the rocks of Vélez de la Gomera, the rocks of Alhucemas, the islands of Chafarinas and Perejil. Spain borders the Atlantic Ocean and Portugal to the west. To the north and the Cantabrian Sea; to the northeast with France and Andorra; on the other side of the Pyrenees; to the east with the Mediterranean Sea, to the south with Morocco, in Ceuta and Melilla on the border of the African continent (Melilla).

Economic activity

Although the primary economic activities of Spain do not have a large share in the total GDP of the economy, they only make up 2.7% of the total economic or productive activity of the country.

In the agricultural production of the country, the production of barley, wheat, oats, rye, sugar beet, fruit and citrus fruits, corn, potatoes, tomatoes, lettuce, legumes and onion is very important, as is the breeding of sheep and pigs. . Climatic and topographic conditions make rainfed agriculture an obligation (Sowing without irrigation) in various parts of the country.

Spain is the European leader in organic farming with 1,317,539 ha, which represents 17% of the Union as a whole.

In the secondary economic activities of the country, the automotive industry stands out. Spain is the ninth largest producer of automobiles in the world and the second in Europe (92% of this production is exported); the production of parts and accessories for vehicles, tires, the pharmaceutical industry, paper and petroleum derivatives.

The agri-food industries such as the production of wines, canned food, dairy products and sausages are also very important.

The tertiary sector is the most important in the economy of Spain, it is the largest economic activity in the country and the one that generates the most jobs.

This sector is composed of education and health services, government, telecommunications, transportation, entertainment, insurance, tourism, retail trade, etc.

They stand out in telecommunications (Telefónica), banking (BBVA and Santander), fashion retail sales (Inditex), insurance (Mapfre) and in hotels and tourism (Sol Melía). Spain is the fourth largest tourist destination in the world and receives more than 53 million visitors a year, thanks to this tourism represents 10% of the country's total GDP.

Government system

Spain's political system is a multi-party system, but since the 1990s two parties have been predominant in politics, the Spanish Socialist Workers' Party (PSOE) and the People's Party (PP). Regional parties, mainly the Basque Nationalist Party (EAJ-PNV), from the Basque Country, and Convergence and Union (CiU) and the Republican Left of Catalonia (ERC), from Catalonia, have also played key roles in Spanish politics. Members of the Congress of Deputies are selected through proportional representation, and the government is formed by the party or coalition that has the confidence of the Congress, usually the party with the largest number of seats. Since the Spanish transition to democracy, there have not been coalition governments; when a party has failed to obtain absolute majority, minority governments have been formed.
